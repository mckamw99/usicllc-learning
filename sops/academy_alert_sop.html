Overview

01

The Academy Alert email template provides a consistent, branded way to communicate system issues, outages, or disruptions affecting USIC Academy users. It mirrors the format of the USIC IT Alert email, adapted for the LAT team's communications.

The template file is an HTML document. Download it, open it in a browser, select all (Ctrl+A / Cmd+A), and paste into Outlook to send.

When to Use

02
✓ Use this template for
  • Docebo LMS outages or degraded performance
  • Course completion or enrollment bugs
  • Power BI / reporting tool disruptions
  • Scheduled maintenance windows
  • Any issue affecting learner access to training
✗ Do not use for
  • Routine announcements or course launches
  • Individual user troubleshooting
  • Non-urgent informational updates
  • Internal LAT team communications

Template Fields

03

Replace every bracketed placeholder before sending. There are six fields to fill in.

[System/Tool Name] Name of the affected system. e.g. Docebo LMS, Power BI, SharePoint
[Brief Issue Description] Short headline description. e.g. Outage, Degraded Performance, Scheduled Maintenance
[Describe the issue] 1–2 sentences explaining what is happening and the scope of impact on users.
[Action steps] What users should do (or not do) while the issue is being resolved. Remove bullet points that don't apply.
[Your Name] Your full name as it should appear in the signature.
[Your Title] Your role title. e.g. Lead Analyst, Learning Analytics & Technology

Step-by-Step

04
1

Download the template

Click the Download Email Template button in the Overview section above. The file will save to your Downloads folder automatically.

Need help finding your downloaded file?
  1. After clicking the download button, look at the bottom of your browser — you may see the file appear there.
  2. If you don't see it, open File Explorer (press Windows key + E) and click Downloads on the left side.
  3. Look for a file named USIC_Academy_Alert_Template.html.
2

Open the file in your browser

Find the downloaded file and double-click it. It will open in your web browser (Chrome or Edge) and display the formatted email.

Need help opening the file?
  1. Go to your Downloads folder and find USIC_Academy_Alert_Template.html.
  2. Double-click the file. It should open automatically in Chrome or Edge and look like a formatted email.
  3. If it opens in Notepad instead, right-click the file → Open with → Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge.
3

Copy the email

With the template open in your browser, press Ctrl+A to select everything, then Ctrl+C to copy it.

Need help selecting and copying?
  1. Click anywhere on the email in your browser to make sure the browser window is active.
  2. Press and hold Ctrl, then press A. Everything on the page should become highlighted in blue.
  3. While still holding Ctrl, press C to copy. Nothing will appear to happen — that's normal, it's been copied to your clipboard.
4

Paste into Outlook

Open a new email in Outlook, click inside the body, and press Ctrl+V to paste. The formatted email — including the logo and colors — will appear.

After pasting, a small clipboard icon will appear near the bottom-right of the pasted content. Click it and choose Keep Source Formatting (the first option) to preserve the logo, colors, and layout.
Need help pasting into Outlook?
  1. Open Outlook and click New Email (top left).
  2. Click once inside the large blank area of the email body.
  3. Press Ctrl+V to paste. The formatted email should appear with the USIC Academy logo and colors.
  4. After pasting, look for a small clipboard icon that appears near the bottom-right of the pasted content. Click it and select Keep Source Formatting (usually the first option). This ensures the colors, logo, and layout are preserved.
  5. If the logo doesn't appear, don't worry — it will show correctly when received by others.

Important: Do not press Ctrl+Z (Undo) after pasting — this can remove the formatting.

5

Edit the placeholders directly in Outlook

Click on any [bracketed placeholder] in the email body and type over it with the correct information — just like editing a Word document. Replace all six placeholders before moving on.

[System/Tool Name]e.g. Docebo LMS, Power BI, SharePoint
[Brief Issue Description]e.g. Outage, Degraded Performance, Scheduled Maintenance
[Describe the issue]1–2 sentences on what is happening and who is affected.
[Action steps]What users should do. Delete any bullet points that don't apply.
[Your Name]Your full name.
[Your Title]Your role, e.g. Lead Analyst, Learning Analytics & Technology
Need help editing the text in Outlook?
  1. Click directly on a bracketed placeholder, e.g. [System/Tool Name]. Your cursor will appear inside the text.
  2. Triple-click to highlight the entire placeholder, then type your replacement text. It will overwrite what was there.
  3. Work through each placeholder one at a time until none remain.
  4. To double-check, use Ctrl+F in Outlook to search for [ — if any brackets are found, you have a placeholder left to fill in.

Tip: If you accidentally delete formatting, close the email without saving and start again from Step 2.

6

Add recipients and send

Academy Alert emails are sent from the shared mailbox, addressed To Undisclosed Recipients, with the company-wide distribution group in BCC.

From learningalerts@usicllc.com
You must have Send As or Send On Behalf access to this mailbox. Contact IT or your manager if you need access added.
To Undisclosed Recipients
Create a contact in Outlook named "Undisclosed Recipients" with the email address learningalerts@usicllc.com and use that as the To recipient.
BCC Everyone-ALL@usicllc.com
If the BCC field is not visible, click Options → BCC in the new email window to enable it.
Subject [System/Tool Name] — [Brief Issue Description]
Match this exactly to the headline in the email body.
To send from a shared mailbox in Outlook: Click Options → From in the ribbon → Other Email Address → type learningalerts@usicllc.com → click OK.
Need help setting up From, To, BCC, and Subject?

Setting the From address:

  1. In the new email window, click the Options tab at the top.
  2. Click From in the ribbon. A From field will appear above the To field.
  3. Click the From dropdown → Other Email Address → type learningalerts@usicllc.com → click OK.

Setting the To address:

  1. In the To field, type Undisclosed Recipients. If Outlook doesn't find it, create it as a contact first:
    • Go to People in Outlook → New Contact.
    • Name: Undisclosed Recipients — Email: learningalerts@usicllc.com.
    • Save, then return and add it to the To field.

Adding the BCC field:

  1. Click the Options tab → click BCC to show the BCC field.
  2. In the BCC field, type Everyone-ALL@usicllc.com.

Final check before sending: From shows learningalerts@usicllc.com • To shows Undisclosed Recipients • BCC shows Everyone-ALL@usicllc.com • Subject matches the email headline • No [brackets] remain in the body.

Tips & Best Practices

06
  • Send alerts as soon as an issue is confirmed — don't wait for a resolution to notify users.
  • Keep the "What's going on?" section to 2–3 sentences maximum. Plain language only.
  • Only include action bullet points that are relevant — delete the ones that don't apply.
  • Send a follow-up "All Clear" email once the issue is resolved using the same template, noting the resolution in the headline.
  • CC or BCC your manager when sending to a broad audience.
  • Match the subject line to the headline: [System/Tool Name] — [Brief Issue Description].
