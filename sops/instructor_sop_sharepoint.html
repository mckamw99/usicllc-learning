Role Overview

01

The Instructor is a Power User role assigned to field-based personnel who deliver in-person training. Instructors can manage ILT sessions, track attendance, and view learner progress within their assigned branch and catalog scope.

Permissions

✓ You CAN
  • View and manage ILT sessions in your catalogs
  • Create and manage your own ILT sessions
  • Mark attendance for sessions you instruct
  • View learner enrollment status
  • Add/remove learners from sessions
  • View completion reports for your sessions
  • Complete your own assigned training
✗ You CANNOT
  • Create or delete courses
  • Edit sessions created by other Power Users
  • Edit course content or SCORM files
  • Create or edit learning plans
  • Modify user profiles or reset passwords
  • Access users outside your assigned branch
  • Delete sessions (requires Training Delivery Manager)

Logging In to Docebo

02

Access the USIC LMS at https://www.usic.training

  1. 1
    Navigate to usic.trainingOpen any web browser and go to https://www.usic.training.
  2. 2
    Single Sign-On (SSO)USIC uses SSO. If you are already logged into your USIC device or Microsoft account, you will be signed in automatically. No separate username or password is required.
  3. 3
    Access Power User featuresAfter login, your admin navigation appears in the left sidebar as a set of icons. Hover over any icon to expand that menu area — you will see significantly more options than a standard learner.
  4. 4
    TroubleshootingIf you cannot log in or cannot see your Instructor features, contact your Training Delivery Manager (TDM) or submit a Zendesk ticket.

Managing ILT Sessions

04
4.1 — Viewing Your Assigned Sessions
  1. 1
    Open the Content & Delivery menuIn the left sidebar, hover over the folder icon to expand the menu, then select Courses.
  2. 2
    Filter by ClassroomFilter by course type 'Classroom' to see ILT courses in your catalogs.
  3. 3
    Find your courseLook for courses in categories assigned to you: Locating (LOC), Safety (EHS), ULE, RECONN, or Leadership Development (LDR).
  4. 4
    Click into the courseOpen the course to see associated sessions, dates, times, locations, and enrolled learners.
4.2 — Marking Attendance
  1. 1
    Navigate to the ILT sessionFrom Content & Delivery › Courses, find the ILT course and click into the specific session you are delivering.
  2. 2
    Open the session rosterClick on the session to view the enrolled learner list.
  3. 3
    Mark status for each learnerFor each learner, select: Attended, Absent, or leave as Enrolled if not yet recorded. Every learner must have a status — do not leave anyone blank.
  4. 4
    Mark the session as CompleteOnce all attendance is recorded, update the session status to Complete.
  5. 5
    Save your changesClick Save. Learners marked Attended will automatically receive course completion credit toward any associated Learning Plans.
⚠ Important: Always mark attendance on the same day as training delivery. Delayed records can cause compliance tracking issues and missed manager notifications.
4.3 — Creating Your Own ILT Session
  1. 1
    Navigate to the ILT courseGo to Content & Delivery › Courses and find the ILT course you want to add a session to.
  2. 2
    Open the Sessions tabClick into the course and select the Sessions tab.
  3. 3
    Click New SessionClick the button to create a new session.
  4. 4
    Complete session detailsEnter the session name, date, start/end time, location (select from the 68 registered USIC locations), and maximum seats.
  5. 5
    Save and notifySave the session. Notify any learners who need to be enrolled and coordinate with your TDM if enrollment needs to be managed in bulk.
Note: You can edit and delete sessions you create. You cannot edit sessions created by other Power Users.
4.4 — Adding or Removing Learners from a Session
  1. 1
    Navigate to the sessionGo to Content & Delivery › Courses › [ILT course] › [session].
  2. 2
    To ADD a learnerClick 'Add User' within the session. Search by name or employee number. Select the learner and confirm enrollment.
  3. 3
    To REMOVE a learnerFind the learner in the session roster. Click the remove/unenroll option next to their name and confirm.
  4. 4
    Notify the learnerIf adding or removing a learner close to the session date, contact them directly — do not rely solely on automated system notifications.

Archiving Enrollments

06

Archiving removes the enrollment from the learner's active view while preserving completion and progress records. Always archive rather than unenroll when a training record needs to be retained.

  1. 1
    Go to Content & Delivery › CoursesFind the course whose enrollments you want to manage.
  2. 2
    Open the courseClick the course title to open its configuration page.
  3. 3
    Open Manage enrollmentsClick the Manage enrollments button in the top right corner.
  4. 4
    Select the user(s)Check the box next to the learner(s) whose enrollment you want to archive.
  5. 5
    Choose action › Archive enrollmentClick Choose action and select Archive enrollment. Confirm. The enrollment will no longer appear in the learner's active course list but the completion record is retained.
Archive vs. Unenroll: Archiving preserves the training record. Unenrolling removes the record entirely — use it only if the enrollment was created in error. If unsure, contact your TDM before unenrolling.
Learning Plans: To archive a learning plan enrollment, go to People › Users › [User] › Learning Plans tab, click the … menu next to the plan, and select Archive enrollment.

Instructor Do's and Don'ts

10
✓ DO
  • Mark attendance on the day of training
  • Record Absent for learners who did not attend — record everyone
  • Verify the session roster before training starts
  • Contact your TDM if you need to edit another user's session
  • Use the employee number to find learners if name search fails
  • Report completion discrepancies promptly to L&D
  • Complete your own assigned training on time
✗ DON'T
  • Leave any learner's attendance status blank
  • Only mark those who attended — record everyone
  • Assume everyone who shows up is enrolled
  • Attempt to edit or delete sessions you did not create
  • Edit learner profile data — Admins only
  • Wait — delays cause compliance tracking issues
  • Share your login credentials with others
